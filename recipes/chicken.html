Fire Chicken

Batter Ingredients

  • ½ cup cornstarch
  • ½ cup flour
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 cup water
  • 10 chicken wings, or drumettes
  • 1 cup cornstarch

Sauce Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • ¼ cup honey
  • 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  • 2 tablespoons gochujang (Korean chili paste)
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, grated
  • sesame seed, to serve

Preperation

  1. Heat oil in a pot to 340°F (170°C).
  2. Mix the ingredients for the batter in a bowl until smooth with no lumps.
  3. Dip a chicken wing into the cornstarch, shaking off excess.
  4. Dredge the wing into the batter, letting excess drip off.
  5. Fry half of the wings at a time for about 5-7 minutes, until light golden. The oil will drop to about 320°F (160°C).
  6. Drain the wings and increase the heat. Heat oil to about 375°F (190°C).
  7. Fry the wings a second time until golden brown and crispy.
  8. Drain and set aside.
  9. In a pan, combine all the ingredients for the sauce and stir until bubbling.
  10. Toss the wings in the sauce.
  11. Enjoy!
